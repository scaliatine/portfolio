printDictionary

A Collaborative Glossary | TXST MFA | David Scaliatine

Scarce, fleeting and evanescent. Continiously circulating and changing, this publication questions permenance and looks to diminish itself of substance. Each time you print this page, the words will circulate and you will encounter a different definition. Print out the series and bind them all together.

printDictionary

Adaptable

Taking the shape of its container, but not limited
to its current form.

A-1

printDictionary

Archive

A collection of original records, documents, books, art, images, etc., put together by individuals, groups, and institutions for preservation. An archive can be used as a primary and secondary source.

A-2

printDictionary

Assemble

The process of collecting, curating, or arranging pieces together to create a whole.

A-3

printDictionary

Circulating

1. To spread objects or ideas to different
places or people.
2. To cycle through physical and digital networks.

C-1

printDictionary

Collective

An act of individuals working together as a group for a short or extended period.

C-2

printDictionary

Container

A space for ideas that can be private or public,
print-based or screen-based, big or small, traditional or experimental.

C-3

printDictionary

Distribution

Share or supply an item to different recipients using either print-based or digital methods and formats.

D-1

printDictionary

Endless Repetition

The process of a design being repeated throughout constant manipulation of variables (materials, size, composition, etc.), ultimately creating endless varieties without changing the original design.

E-1

printDictionary

Ephemeral

The concept of an impermanent, fleeting moment.

E-2

printDictionary

Haecceity

Individual attributes that make an object
unique or different from others.

H-1

printDictionary

Hybrid Publications

Book projects that include both a print and web-based component, and explore the relationship between the two versions. A hybrid publication could also describe any book project that incorporates two or more publishing methods.

H-2

printDictionary

Manipulate Content

The reiterating or editing of original content
for different media use and platforms.

M-1

printDictionary

Nonlinearity

The act of moving randomly and obscurely through all directions with no single meaning.

N-1

printDictionary

Print to Web
Web to Print

The act of developing a digital counterpart of a printed series or creative work for online viewing,
or vice versa with a digital series or creative work published for physical viewing.

P-1

printDictionary

Fluid

An idea or design that can be adaptable, flexible,
and restricted to a specific format.

F-1

printDictionary

Reproduction

Making a copy of a piece of work or content, either using another person's art or a reiteration of the original work.

R-1

printDictionary

Retranslate

To give a new meaning or explanation
to the original form.

R-2

printDictionary

Reverential

A feeling or attitude of deep respect,
tinged with awe.

R-3

printDictionary

Tactility

An essence of physicality, palpability
and ability to be felt.

T-1

printDictionary

Vibrational Qualities

The characteristics of an object or image that comes from it being fluid, able to exist in various states, and occupy multiple positions simultaneously.

V-1

Modernity is the transient, the fleeting, the contingent; it is one half of art, the other being the eternal and the immovable.
